# Brand Philosophy

## The name

**SVNT** is Simon SVNT — the founder and curator. **SUPPLY** signals intention: this is a supply of things worth having, not a marketplace of everything.

The name is deliberately abbreviated. It reads like a brand mark, not a sentence. It works at small sizes and large. It's opaque enough to be memorable — you either know what it means or you ask.

---

## Red and white

The brand palette is intentionally stark:

- **White** — the canvas. Product images sit on white backgrounds. The storefront feels clean, minimal, gallery-like. Nothing competes with the products.
- **Red** — the accent. Used exclusively for interactive elements: buttons, links, hover states, the saved heart, the search underline. It's the only color that breaks the monochrome.

This restraint serves the editorial voice. The products are colorful — the interface is not. Red acts as a visual pointer: "this is where you act." It's bold, confident, and editorial rather than corporate.

**What it's not:** A flag reference. The palette choice is aesthetic, not national.

---

## What "curated" actually means here

SVNT SUPPLY does not scrape product feeds or auto-populate its catalog. Every product is:

1. **Selected by hand** — an admin creates each product manually, writes editorial text, uploads images
2. **Written about** — every product has a verdict, pros/cons breakdown, durability score, "who it's for / who it's not for" sections
3. **Released intentionally** — products go live through the Sunday Drop system, not on-demand

This is a magazine that links to products, not a store that writes blurbs. The editorial voice matters more than the transaction.

---

## Design principles

### 1. The product is the hero

Product images are cut out from backgrounds, rendered large, and given breathing room. Card and hero surfaces use fixed editorial photography ratios, not standard ecommerce thumbnails.

### 2. Nothing auto-plays, nothing pops up

Animations are subtle and gesture-driven. There is no autoplay carousel, no exit-intent popup (the newsletter signup is inline), no countdown timer. The interface respects attention.

### 3. Desktop-first, mobile-capable

The admin panel is desktop-only. The storefront works on mobile but is designed for desktop browsing. The assumption is that curation-heavy shopping happens on larger screens.

### 4. The catalog is small on purpose

The catalog is browseable in minutes. The intent is not to be comprehensive — it's to be a reliable filter. If it's on SVNT SUPPLY, someone already decided it's worth your time.
